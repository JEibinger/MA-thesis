    Introduction Graz, Ms. 1609 Wien, Cod. 4995 München, Clm 15632

Position placement of long-s and round-s in the manuscript Gr1

A larger dataset provides a more suitable base for paleographic comparison. The Gr1 manuscript contains far more folios than the B2, namely 168 with 3867 lines.

The manuscript contains 5156 long-s and a little more than half as many round-s, 2620.

Double s and combinations

The double s is a common occurence in German medieval manuscripts.
But how are these occurences constituted? What combinations of s are the most frequent?

To answer these questions, the next charts show the distribution between double long-s, double round-s and the combination of both as well as in case of combination which one leads more often.

The distribution is very uneven.

Of 744 double s occurences overall, 491 consist of only long-s while only 9 are a double round-s. The combination of the two in a double constellation is 244.

Meanwhile, no round-s is leading a combo constellation - all s combinations have a long-s as the leading character.

While interesting, these findings are not surprising when the previously estimated position placement is taken into account. As the prevalent s in the final but not medial position, the round-s simply has not as much potential for double s constellations.

Character occurences with long-s and round-s

Another interesting aspect is the occurence of characters next to the types of s. Most importantly the characters "ch" as well as "t" as they form a significant phonem and consonant cluster in combination with s.

The overwhelming number of character combinations with "t" and "ch" occur with long-s.

The placement of long-s across folios in Gr1

Except for the end of the manuscript, the distribution of position for long-s are not remarkable. The handshift, which occurs at the 69th folio is not noticeable.

Starting at the 152nd folio there is a remarkable drop in initial long-s.

The placement of round-s across folios in Gr1

Comparing the two graphs, we notice a significant drop in final round-s around the same folios, but a rise in initial round-s.

Initial and medial s across folios in Gr1

Comparing the development of both s in the initial and medial positions show, that the round-s supplements the initial position like nowhere else in the entire manuscript.

The transcription shows, that the folios in question contain Latin text as well as lists of dishes.
