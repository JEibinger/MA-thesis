Transcription in CoReMA

The digital edition of the German language cooking recipe collections aims at providing a hyperdiplomatic transcription of the source texts. This kind of research output is meant to provide our own and other disciplines with source texts further research can be built upon.

The following visualisations are based on data analysis using Python, the libraries pandas and NumPy as well as X-technologies.

Position placement of the long s in the manuscript B2

For decades the long s was omitted in editions of German medieval manuscripts as the graph was dismissed as a mere notation variant of the same phonem the round s is corresponding to and therefore not worth the effort in these (typewriter-)times. This is no longer an excuse as digital methods and digital editions provide the necessary tools and are available to everyone who it may concern to provide a accurate edition of the texts. Furthermore, the correct and detailed transcription allows us to find and compare all notations.

Position placement of long s and round s per position

The roles of long s and round s within the word boundaries are more or less agreed upon in the modern scientific literature. While the long s should be fixed to initial and medial positions, the round s is primarily used in the final position. This assumption can be further examined thanks to the large hyper-diplomatically transcribed corpus of the CoReMA project.

In the short manuscript of B2, the round s occurs in the initial position 15 times. Because it is so short, this analysis is not yet very meaningful, but functioned as a trial run for the analysis method - further analysis on larger manuscript collections offers a more substantial basis for assumptions.
