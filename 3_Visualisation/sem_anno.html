Semantic annotations in CoReMA

The semantic annotation of text passages, phrases or single words in the recipes is done manually on the basis of a normalized version of the recipe texts, resulting from the transcription. It lays the foundation for the comparison of the cooking recipe texts handed down in various languages. At this stage Early New High German, Middle French, and Medieval Latin texts are analysed for text relationships, dependencies, and variance between manuscripts.

Although some manuscripts annotations already contain the macrostructure, the following visualisations focus only on ingredients, tools and dishes for the moment.

Comparison of mentioned fats

The FAO categories suggest certain approaches to analysis. One is the comparison of the mentioned ingredients in the recipes, that share the same category. Fats are used to fry, roast and enhance flavour - also in medieval recipes. But which kind of fat was used the most?

Comparison of mentioned spices overall

The explicit use of spices in the recipes is an interesting topic. Most spices are not mentioned by name, rather the advice is to add just that - spice.

The second most mentioned - saffron surpisingly enough - is mentioned only half as many times, closely followed in numbers by third and fourth place, table salt and vinegar.

From close reading the recipes, we know vinegar is mainly used in sauce recipes and to add a tangy flavour in general.
